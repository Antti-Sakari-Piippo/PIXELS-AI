Frequently Asked Questions

What is Pixels AI Image Generator?

AI generates images by using deep learning models, particularly generative adversarial networks (GANs) or variational autoencoders (VAEs). These models are trained on large datasets of images and learn the underlying patterns and features. When generating images, the AI model uses random input and transforms it into output that resembles the patterns it has learned, resulting in new, original images.

What is the role of blockchain?

Blockchain plays a pivotal role in Pixels by verifying the authenticity of digital art, providing the framework for smart contracts to automate transactions, decentralizing control to eliminate intermediaries, and ensuring transparency through an immutable ledger.

How do I transfer image ownership?

To transfer ownership of a digital image on Pixels, you can employ blockchain's smart contract functionality, which involves specifying the recipient's wallet address and executing the transaction, effectively changing ownership.

Are there any fees for transactions?

Yes, there are transaction fees associated with blockchain transactions. These fees can vary depending on network usage and the complexity of the transaction, and there might be additional platform-specific fees for listing and selling digital art.

What happens if Pixels shuts down?

In the event of Pixels shutting down, the decentralized nature of the blockchain ensures that the ownership and transaction history of digital art remains intact, and users can continue to access their digital assets and provenance information.

What if I lose access to my tokens?

If you lose access to your tokens, it can be challenging to recover them. To mitigate this risk, it's crucial to secure your private keys, use robust storage methods, and, in the event of lost access, consider reaching out to the blockchain platform's support for guidance.
